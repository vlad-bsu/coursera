After you turn the kettle on, temperature $$Temp$$ starts to increase over time. After time $$T$$ level of water is $$L$$. Since water is boiling, water level can be considered to be random. You also know the height of a kettle. If at some point water level exceeds this value, water will split on a table. We will denote this event as a binary random variable $$O$$ (overflow). Our goal is to determine the maximum allowed initial water level $$L_{max}$$ and to write it down in a kettle manual. Normally we would like to find $$L_{max}$$ for which, for example, $$P(O\,|\,L_0 = L_{max}) = 0.1%$$.
