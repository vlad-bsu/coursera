Question 6

Suppose we are given a classification task. In a simple two model linear mix we usually use weights $$\alpha$$ for the first model and $$\beta$$ for the second one. The coefficients are usually chosen such that $$\alpha \beta = 1$$, because convex combination of probability vectors is a probability vector.

Still, sometimes it is beneficial to tune $$\alpha$$ and $$beta$$ independently, e.g. mix with $$\alpha=0.1$$ and $$\beta=0.8$$ works best.

However, for some metrics it never makes sense to tune $$\alpha$$ and $$\beta$$ independently. That is, searching for independent $$\alpha$$ and $$\beta$$ will never give you better results than searching for weights, constrained to be $$\beta = 1 - \alpha$$. Select such metrics.
